FILIP MALISZEWSKI

My motivation behind the airport assignment was strictly ruled by the properties of the screen as well as the client itself. In this case client is not actually the airport, but people in the waiting room, stressing out or relaxing before the flight. To actually understand the viewer is to put oneself into their place, as I did since I’m an international student who flies back to my home country few times a year. The person watching, in that case, doesn’t want to be immersed into deep understanding of social dilemmas and won’t be focusing on the artwork itself for a longer period of time, unless the work would be constantly visually stimulating. The screen itself is a fascinating case of cutting edge technology that us as artist are able to experience and experiment with. Even though movie has to be silent and the colour scheme monochrome (almost black and white). The advantage of having a see through display is a perk as well as a challenge itself.
While editing, a happy accident happened as the bird from one frame crossed into another turning into the car. That accident shaped my idea and made it full. Beginning of the movie is constructed in the way the videos on each screen overlap. They create a singular movie screen despite presenting completely diﬀerent clips. And that is also the most entertaining part for the viewer. No matter how many times they watch the movie which is only 30 seconds long, they will want to rewatch it. I tried it with audience consisting of my friends, peers, sometimes random people and the thing linking all of them together is rewatching the movie in the loop, always finding new connections. I think that will work out in the airport environment perfectly, especially for people with anxiety and kids, as it is almost interactive in that form. Almost like adventure point-and-click games I used to adore as a child, actively engaging the viewer in looking for the clues.
