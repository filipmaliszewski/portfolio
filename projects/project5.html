FILIP MALISZEWSKI

A competition was organized within the Willem de Kooning Academie to create a teaser for the short film program of the International Film Festival Rotterdam (IFFR). As students, we were given just two weeks to complete the project - one week to develop and pitch an idea to IFFR representatives, and the second to finalize it. I began with a simple concept: commuting to the festival by train, a setting Iâ€™ve always appreciated as a liminal space. The train also reminded me of cinema - the interior is typically dimmer than the passing view outside, which is constantly in motion. There's also the rhythmic soundscape of machinery, which enhances the sensory experience. After receiving positive feedback, I began illustrating each frame by hand and built a short narrative around them. The audio was a soundscape I composed using field recordings from my daily commute to school. In the end, my project won and was screened at IFFR, preceding the short film program.
